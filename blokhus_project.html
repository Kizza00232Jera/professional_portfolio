Blokhus Sports and Events

Product: Responsive dynamic webpage

My role: User research, Web Design, Front-end developer

View website
View Code

Problem and the task

The main issue that Blokhus Sport and Events community is facing, is that all the information for different sports and events is spread out on Facebook pages which makes it difficult to get an overview for the potential visitors of Blokhus. Another limitation that Blokhus Sport and Events is facing is that the only way the people can inform themselves for past events is from the local newspaper. Blokhus Sport and Events is exploring possibilities of attracting new members by creating the membership status with which you can participate in multiple sports and events that are cost-friendly and with more benefits.

Our task was to dynamically display the upcoming events in an understandable manner and display stories from past events as well as a good introduction to other offers from Blokhus Sport & Events.

The project group decided to use a Content Management System (CMS) - WordPress. Project duration was 4 weeks.

Our process

We started the project by doing online research about who might be potential visitors of Blokhus webpage; people within Denmark, and tourists from countries such as Germany, Norway and Sweden (adventure seekers and active/interested in sports and sport events).

That helped us to categorize and interview the right people who can give us valid information that helped us to improve quality of the website. Interviews were perfomed following the interview guide and then Empathy Maps were created so that we can understand our user's needs. After that, personas are created with a help of gathered information from research and interviews. Both personas have two different situations of how they might use Blokhus webpage and two scenarios on how they feel and react using it.

We started prototyping in multiple iterations moving progressively rom low-fidelity prototypes(sketches) to high-fidelity prototypes. Visual hierarchy and the overall layout was tested using 5-second test. We used Figma as a development tool because it provides shared artboards where it can be collaborated. For building the product, atomic design was used to structure the layout and elements in the page.

High Fidelity prototype

HTML, CSS and JavaScript are used, as well as headless CMS (WordPress) for building structure of articles. There was focus on the on-site and off-site SEO which we tried to improve by creating content with relevant keywords, availability to update or add more content(to give visitors reason to keep coming back), having content with proper headings, optimized images, font sizes and typography, and making page responsive for different screen sizes and devices with meta tags included on every page. At the end, we validated the code, tested its readability and page reloading speed.
