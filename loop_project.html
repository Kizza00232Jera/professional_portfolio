Loop Fitness Microsite

Product: Responsive microsite made with grids

My role: User research, Web Design, Front-end developer

View website
View Code

Problem and the task

The problem that Loop Fitness faces every year is that they get overcrowded in january (after Christmas and New Year holidays) because they have campaign for 1 DKK every autumn and winter.

Our task was to create microsite where we encourage people to start their fitness journey earlier, during November, not wait until they get out of shape to take care of their physical and mental health and to show people why Loop Fitness is the right place to head start an active lifestyle.

Our process

We started the project by getting to know the client in order to plan and conduct the user research. The team prepared for a client meeting by doing a partial content inventory of Loop's main website to gain insight on the client's values.

After that, we did interviews where target people were from Denmark and over the age of 30 (that was information we gained from the meeting with the company). We decided to split those people in two groups, the first group were those who already workout and the second those who don't.

We started design process by brainstorming session where we all came up with layout ideas in form of paper prototypes (sketches) and wireframes in Adobe XD. After that we combined our ideas into one high-fidelity prototype. The color palette was taken from Loop Fitness' main website and the navigation was also inspired by what they already had at that time. We also decided to have part dedicated to the Christmas.

High Fidelity prototype

All the content was organized in a grid system. The grid consisted of two big columns for the image and text with a gap between and empty space before and after the content.
